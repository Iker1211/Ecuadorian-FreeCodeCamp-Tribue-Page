José Raul Capablanca

Chess prodigy José Raúl Capablanca revolutionized the game with his exceptional endgame mastery and lightning-fast play

José Raul Capablanca in front of the chess board.

Here are some important points about his career and life.

  • Early Chess Prodigy: Capablanca’s chess talent emerged early. By age four, he was already observing his father play, and at 12, he defeated the Cuban champion, Juan Corzo.
  • San Sebastián Victory: His victory over Frank Marshall in the 1911 San Sebastián tournament catapulted him into the international chess scene.
  • World Championship Match: Capablanca’s 1921 world championship win against Emanuel Lasker marked a significant milestone in his career.
  • Undefeated Streak: From February 10, 1916, to March 21, 1924, Capablanca remained undefeated in serious games—a remarkable achievement.
  • Alekhine’s Challenge: In 1927, he faced Alexander Alekhine in a world championship match and lost the title.
  • Retirement and Comeback: Capablanca withdrew from competitive chess in 1931 but returned in 1934.
  • Endgame Mastery: His endgame skills were legendary, and he authored the influential book “Chess Fundamentals.”
  • Influence on Future Champions: Capablanca’s style inspired players like Bobby Fischer and Anatoly Karpov.
  • Legacy: His legacy endures as one of the greatest chess players in history.
  • Tragic Passing: Capablanca’s life was cut short when he suffered a brain hemorrhage in 1942.
